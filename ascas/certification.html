The idea of 1 Star approval for pet products is that they should be of a standard that would allow them to be sold in market as cosmetic personal care products. (You would expect that products you use on your pets should be safe enough for you to use as well!). For a brand to be accepted for 1 Star they must supply a full formulation break down and specification that will be independently checked to make sure it is safe. They must also show details of challenge, stability testing and final packaging (see details below). Brief details of what a product needs to comply to are:

  • Only allowed to use ingredients permitted in current EU Cosmetic regulations
  • Products must not contain any ingredients directly derived from animals. Ingredients harvested from animals i.e. honey or milk are permitted.
  • Products must have been through stability testing to show that the product will be stable for at least a year after date of manufacture
  • Products containing water must have had a challenge test (to show that the product will be adequately preserved)
  • Packaging must display the following
    • An ingredient list using inci names and to the same EU cosmetics regulations that personal care cosmetics follow. (Ingredients must be listed in descending order of their use level. If this is under 1%, they can be in any order after the materials at 1% or more).
    • Best before date
    • Address and contact details of brand/manufacturer
    • Country of origin

The aim of 2 Stars products is that they are milder and kinder to skin than 1 Star products and are therefore suited for use with puppies and dogs with sensitive skin. 2 Stars products as a start must follow all guidelines set out for 1 Star. The only change is a restriction of ingredients that can be used. 2 Stars products must not contain any of the following ingredients: Alkyl Sulfates – namely the below

  • Sodium Lauryl Sulfate
  • Sodium Laureth Sulfate
  • Ammonium Lauryl Sulfate
  • Ammonium Laureth Sulfate
  • Sodium Coco Sulfate
  • TEA Lauryl Sulfate
  • MEA Lauryl Sulfate

C12-14 Olefin Sulphonate
Paraben based preservatives
Isothiazolinone based preservatives
Cocamide DEA

The aim of 3 Stars is that the products are natural and good for the environment. As you would expect the list of ingredients that are not allowed in 3 stars is greater (listed below). In addition to this to comply with 3 stars, products must adhere to the 1 Star specification plus the following:

  • Ingredients used must be GMO free (allowing up to 1% cross contamination that is shown by PCR analysis)
  • No nanoparticles allowed
  • Product must contain at least 10% organic ingredients/and or 2% Fairtrade ingredients.
  • Packaging must use either biodegradable or recyclable packaging
  • Only use ingredients that are allowed by BUAV (British Union for the Abolition of Vivisection). BUAV peaceful campaigns have had some positive results within in the EU.
    • 2004 Complete ban on testing of finished cosmetic products on animals.
    • 2009 Total ban on testing cosmetic ingredients on animals within the EU.

Banned Ingredients list

  • Alkyl Sulfates
  • Sulphonates
  • Paraben based preservatives
  • Isothiazolinone based preservatives
  • Bronopol based preservatives
  • Formaldehyde releasing preservatives
  • Cocamide DEA
  • Cocamide MEA
  • Cocamide MIPA
  • Synthetic polymers – (ie Acrylates Copolymers and Carbopols)
  • PEG-based ingredients
  • Ethoxylated ingredients
  • Polysorbates
  • Petrochemical based ingredients
  • Silicones
  • Synthetic dyes
  • Synthetic fragrances
